# Customer acquisition plan for SignalSift

## Constraint
This business only counts if it can find paying subscribers, not just produce clever alerts.

## Beachhead ICP
Start with **small marketing agencies** and **fractional lead-gen shops** serving SMBs.

Why:
- they already prospect constantly
- they understand the value of timing and signal-based outreach
- one customer can monitor many target accounts
- they can justify $49-$149/month quickly if alerts produce meetings

## Core offer
"Know which prospects just became more likely to buy."

## First acquisition channels
1. **Cold outbound to agencies**
   - message owners with 1-2 real example alerts from businesses in their niche
   - CTA: free 7-day monitored watchlist for 10 accounts
2. **Founder-led direct demos**
   - short Loom-style walkthroughs once live deployment exists
3. **Niche landing pages**
   - SignalSift for agencies
   - SignalSift for SEO agencies
   - SignalSift for paid media / web / CRO agencies
4. **Communities**
   - agency owner and operator communities where prospecting leverage matters

## Offer design
- free sample alerts for 5-10 companies
- paid plan once they want ongoing monitoring
- annual discount after first successful month

## Why someone buys
- better outreach timing
- fewer wasted outbound touches
- easier client reporting for agencies
- differentiated prospecting angle

## First proof points to chase
- alert opened
- lead saved/exported
- outreach sent because of a signal
- meeting booked from a signal

## Metrics
- visitor -> trial
- trial -> paid
- alert -> saved lead
- saved lead -> outreach sent
- outreach sent -> meeting booked

## Biggest go-to-market risk
If alerts are too generic, users won't pay.

## Mitigation
- start with one niche
- make signals concrete and legible
- show example outreach hooks, not just raw diffs
