# 3300 Darien Dr — Presenter Notes (Luxury / Analytical Tone)

## Overall tone
- polished
- calm
- premium
- analytical beneath the surface
- never defensive
- confident without sounding rigid

## What this presentation needs to do
1. Make the seller feel that Chris sees the home as premium.
2. Show that the pricing thought process is analytical, not arbitrary.
3. Preserve room for a premium pricing discussion.
4. Avoid sounding like the seller is being "priced down" too early.
5. Keep the recommendation slide seller-facing rather than internal/agent-facing.

## Core themes to repeat
- positioning matters as much as pricing
- luxury buyers compare emotionally and analytically
- this home has more character than many newer alternatives
- the right strategy depends on the seller's priorities
- premium presentation can justify a premium conversation

## Verbal emphasis points
- school and lifestyle corridor
- entertaining quality
- French-inspired character
- recent investment and stewardship
- difference between a premium launch strategy and a market-aligned strategy

## Important strategic framing
When discussing $2.15M, do **not** present it as a guaranteed market value conclusion.
Present it as a **premium positioning strategy** designed to:
- elevate the home's stature
- preserve negotiating room
- align with seller psychology
- remain within a more supportable upper band of the market

## Suggested phrasing
- "premium positioning strategy"
- "upper-tier launch"
- "market-aligned alternative"
- "competition-driven alternative"
- "introduced with intention"
- "high-character luxury home"
- "architectural warmth"
- "negotiation room"

## Avoid saying
- "the comps don't support it"
- "it might be overpriced"
- "we can always reduce"
- "it's really worth less but..."

## Better alternatives
Instead of: "the comps don't support it"
Say: "The strongest analytical support sits lower, but a premium launch can still be justified as a strategic positioning choice."

Instead of: "it might be overpriced"
Say: "A premium strategy can produce a different pacing and negotiation dynamic than a market-aligned strategy."

Instead of: "we can always reduce"
Say: "Each pricing path creates a different buyer response profile, and it is best to choose the one that aligns with your goals from the start."
