# Luxury seller intake — recommended structure

## Positioning
This should feel like a confidential pre-listing property profile, not a generic questionnaire.

## Purpose
Capture everything public records miss so pricing, positioning, and presentation reflect the home's true value.

## Sections
1. Property basics
2. Purchase and ownership history
3. Improvements and upgrades
4. Interior features and finishes
5. Exterior, lot, and setting
6. Systems, technology, and maintenance
7. Community and lifestyle value
8. Pricing, timing, and seller priorities

## Why this structure works
- starts easy and factual
- moves into upgrades and value-driving details
- captures both hard data and narrative selling points
- ends with seller priorities and likely comp/perception inputs

## Important notes
- Luxury sellers often underestimate how important finish-level detail is for pricing strategy.
- Asking about costs and dates of improvements helps support later value arguments.
- Asking what they believe sets the home apart gives useful language for the presentation.
